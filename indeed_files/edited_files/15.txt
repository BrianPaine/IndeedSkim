SUMMARY OF JOB DESCRIPTION

The Data Analytics Developer/Analyst is a valuable part of the Business Technology Applications Team. The successful candidate will work in a team environment to provide modeling of complex business problems, discovering business insights and identifying opportunities through the use of statistical, algorithmic, mining and visualization techniques. In addition to advanced analytic skills, this individual is also proficient at integrating and preparing large, varied datasets, architecting specialized databases and computing environments, and communicating results.

The Data Analytics Developer/Analyst should be adept at knowledge discovery and data inference and exploration, as well as predictive and prescriptive analytics approaches. This often involves employing a variety of techniques for leveraging troves of information to reveal hidden insight that lies beneath the surface. They must also be adept at applying analytic techniques and integrating ad hoc data sources.

In addition, the successful candidate must be a self-starter that pursues self-education, is customer focused, supports the organization's goals and values, and is willing to work extended hours as necessary. The successful candidate is expected to travel with a laptop computer and other necessary peripherals. The expectation is that if hired the employee will take laptop home each night for system support and disaster recovery purposes.

DESCRIPTION OF ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS

DATA SYSTEM REQUIREMENTS



Proven data analytic skills, pattern recognition, math, and statistics are required.

Must possess complex modeling skills to solve business problems.

Programming skills in SQL, R, Python and similar tools.

Must possess good communication skills with solid business acumen in order to describe the value of findings to the business.

Understanding of and ability to develop machine learning algorithms.

Must possess business solution experience.

Understanding of application and database architecture.

The basic understanding of "what's next" and having the vision to bring ideas forward to be shared openly.

Understanding and adherence to least privilege methodology.

Understanding of a virtual architecture, including virtual delivery of applications and desktops would be helpful.

Fundamental understanding of Windows server operating systems.

Must follow defined processes to resolve incidents, requiring the use of incident management system. 

Perform off-hour maintenance work on nights and weekends.

Follow change management procedures and policies in developing and implementing changes.



COLLEAGUE SUPPORT



Strong employee service skills required.

Ability to tell an employee no is essential.

Respond to requests via telephone, in person, email, or help desk tickets.

Respond timely to requests, resolving issues, and documenting changes within established parameters and escalating those which need higher level review.



GENERAL BUSINESS TECHNOLOGY SUPPORT



Adhere to Northwestern Energy's Professional Knowledge Worker Competencies.

Comply with internal controls, policies, practices, procedures, and standards.

Excellent oral and written communication skills. 

Work with other BT staff on projects and troubleshooting problems.

Understanding of network infrastructure, including all layers of the Open Systems Interconnection (OSI) model. 

Understanding of the Confidentiality, Integrity and Availability (CIA) Triad.



GENERAL QUALIFICATIONS



Bachelor's degree from four-year accredited college with some related experience or equivalent combination of education and experience.

Must be of the highest character.

Ability to develop positive colleague, team, and leadership relationships to facilitate Business Technology business partnerships.

Must be able to work in a team environment and adapt to changing responsibilities and priorities.

Must be able to continually learn and enhance technical skills and knowledge.

Must have strong organization skills to coordinate workload and individual development.

Must have strong written and oral communication skills.

Occasional travel will be required (day trips and overnight).

May be required to serve in an on-call rotation.

Must be a free thinker with the ability to find and present creative solutions to varying business challenges.
