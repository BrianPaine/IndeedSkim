Data Scientist
Arlington, Virginia, United States &middot; Strategic Initiatives

DescriptionReporting to the Chief Strategy Officer, the Data Scientist position is charged with helping to build theorganization’s data analytic and research function. The individual will be responsible for all aspects ofthe research function, from facilitating the movement of data between source and analytical data storesto producing analyses and reports (written and visual) in support of organizational priorities and strategic initiatives.We are looking for a mission-driven, collaborative, and creative data scientist with strong communicationskills and a passion for education, data, and technology. This is an ideal position for a hardworking,adaptable, self-directed, and innovative individual looking to work in a collegial workplace, learning from,with, and among smart and fun professionals.Essential responsibilities: Perform data extracts and loads from source data systems to analytical data stores. Implement and maintain systems and processes to aggregate, store, and analyze data. Complete ad hoc data extract and reporting tasks as requested by organizational leaders. Work with large &amp; growing educational datasets using descriptive statistics and data visualization tools. Create insights from existing data, and drive the collection of new data through instrumentation, surveys, and other tools. Collaborate with internal stakeholders to understand results and put insights into operation. Create reproducible, documented analyses that lend themselves to automation. Support executive and board level reporting via dashboards. Create &amp; improve predictive models in support of organization programs. Assist in writing and presenting summaries of research and statistical analyses to internal stakeholders as well as external communities. Collaborate with others in organization to design and conduct research studies to inform initiatives relating to Common App strategic priorities, member engagement, and institutional citizenship in the higher education community. Models and upholds company core values. Performs other related duties and responsibilities as assigned. Requirements Master’s degree in a quantitative discipline, or a Bachelor’s degree and 5-7 years related experience. At least 3 years experience working among the following functional domains: data reporting, SQL writing, database schema design (OLTP or OLAP), ETL, data analytics, data mining, and data quality. Experience with AWS data technologies (e.g., RDS, DynamoDB, Redshift, Kinesis, QuickSight, CloudSearch). Fluent coding ability with Python and SQL required. Experience coping with imperfect data. Facility with relational, columnar, and NoSQL database systems. Experience with descriptive statistics and data visualization in R and software like Tableau. Experience with log aggregation software and Google Analytics is also desirable. Knowledge of National Center for Education Statistics, Census, and/or consumer datasets is preferred. Project work in designing and implementing data reporting and analytic systems, including both front-end self service reporting as well as backend technologies. Strong interpersonal skills and service orientation. Passion for and proven ability for turning raw data into knowledge through compelling and understandable reports and visuals. Must be authorized to work lawfully in the United States. BenefitsWe value our employees’ time and efforts. Our commitment to your success is enhanced by ourcompetitive salary and an extensive benefits package including paid time off, medical, dental and visionbenefits and future growth opportunities within the organization. Plus, we work to maintain the bestpossible environment for our staff, where people can learn and grow. We strive to provide acollaborative, creative workplace where each person feels encouraged to contribute to our processes,decisions, planning and culture.
