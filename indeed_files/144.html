Description

Pierce Transit is recruiting for a Data Analyst to join the Data Analytics team in Lakewood.

This is an exceptional opportunity to join a highly respected team of dedicated professionals. If you have experience utilizing data to prepare reports, proposals, and dashboards, verifying and controlling data for accuracy and consistency, and performing complex data analysis; then this might be just the right position for you.

As an integral part of the Data Analytics Team, this person will work with the Business Data Analytics Administrator to understand agency business questions, define the appropriate metrics and support the reporting of those metrics.

This position requires a team player able to develop metrics that provide actionable insights, using efficient, scalable and repeatable processes. A primary function of the position will be collecting and performing complex analysis of Agency data. This person is relied on to ensure data quality in all metrics used in reporting, including metrics in dashboards or other tools that can be accessed by others. Where possible, this role is responsible for implementing automated or self-service solutions.

Essential Functions

The following functions are not intended to serve as a comprehensive list of all duties performed  in this classification, only a representative summary of the primary duties and responsibilities. Incumbent(s) may not be required to perform all duties listed and may be required to perform additional, position-specific duties.

  • Preparing dashboards, reports, and operational process documentation;
  • Creating reports and graphical representations from a variety of data sources and effectively troubleshooting for data inaccuracies and data quality issues;
  • Reviewing data management process and proposing improvements and efficiencies whenever possible;
  • Ensuring rigorous quality control over data, analysis and metrics;
  • Applying critical thinking to available agency data to create metrics that provide actionable insights;
  • Performing duties in support of special and ongoing projects;
  • Creating and presenting context rich analytical results;
  • Attending a variety of meetings and serving on committees as required;
  • Working collaboratively with Agency personnel to resolve problems and coordinate work efforts;
  • Maintaining technical and professional knowledge by attending seminars, reviewing professional publications, taking classes and participating in development activities;
  • Operating as an analytic resource to other departments with a 'customer service' mind-set;
  • Responding to ad-hoc analytical requests as directed;
  • Performing other duties as assigned.

Qualifications

To be successful in this role, the individual will be expected to have knowledge of:

  • Reporting/data mining methodologies;
  • Quality control and assurance principles;
  • Data validation and record-keeping techniques;
  • Familiarity with an operations organization.
  • Statistics and statistical concepts;
  • Researching and analyzing complex data via tables, graphs, and written analysis;
In addition to the knowledge listed above, the incumbent will also be required to possess the ability to:
  • Apply critical thinking and interpretation;
  • Analyze complex, multi-dimensional data sets;
  • Utilize relational databases;
  • Extract accurate conclusions and identify trends from quantitative information;
  • Troubleshoot data inaccuracies;
  • Maintain focused attention on details;
  • Exercise appropriate discretion; business judgment and time management.
  • Learn new concepts and technology;
  • Develop working relationships across multiple organizational functions;
  • Demonstrate good interpersonal skills when interacting with others;
  • Communicate effectively verbally and in writing;
  • Demonstrate at least intermediate to expert level experience using MS Excel
  • Have experience in MS Power BI or similar reporting tools;
  • Work in cross-functional teams.
To be considered, applicants must meet the following minimum qualifications:
  • An Associates Degree in a quantitative field such as finance, business (and)
  • Three (3) years general management support to include two (2) or more years of experience in a data analytics role
          Or
  • An equivalent combination of related education and experience sufficient to successfully perform the essential duties of the job listed above, will be accepted to meet the minimum qualifications.
Ideal qualifications include:
  • Prior experience in transit
  • Experience in Microsoft Power BI report development 
Application Instructions:
Complete the electronic application. Submit a resume and cover letter that clearly conveys your experience and qualifications as they relate to this vacancy.

Paid Leave:
Full-time Pierce Transit employees receive 12 paid holidays, 12 days of sick leave, and 12 or 13 (provisional union affiliation) vacation days per year.  Part-time employees, with the exception of Relief Transit Operators, receive pro-rated leave, to include sick leave, vacation, and holiday pay.  Relief Transit Operators are eligible for 6 paid days of vacation leave after six months of employment which can be used in hourly, daily, or weekly increments as well as two personal holidays.  Relief transit operators are eligible for a pro-rated rate of sick leave upon hire, but do not receive holiday pay.  Leave accrual rate may increase based upon longevity with Pierce Transit.

Insurance Plans:
Pierce Transit employees and their dependents (including domestic partners) may elect a medical and dental plan. Employees may also participate in a flexible spending account for eligible health care and dependent care expenses.  Medical benefits begin the first day of the month following date of hire and dental benefits begin the first day of the seventh month of employment.

Retirement Plan:
All employees of Pierce Transit are required to participate in the Washington State Public Employees' Retirement System (PERS).  Employees are not covered under the Social Security System except for a 1.45% payment for Medicare.

Deferred Compensation Plan: 
Employees are eligible to participate in a 457 savings plan with an employer match of up to 4.25% of annual compensation.

Hours of Work:
Most Pierce Transit employees have a 40-hour workweek; however, positions may require shift work, overtime, and weekend work.  Flexible schedules are available in some departments.

Probationary Period:
All regular employees are subject to successful completion of a probationary period of six months, unless the position is listed as "at-will."

Union Affiliation:
Job classifications covered by union security provisions require union membership and payment of dues and fees.
