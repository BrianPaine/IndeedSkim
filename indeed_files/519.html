Requisition Number 17-0270
Post Date 9/13/2017
Title Predictive Modeler
Location Corporate Headquarters
City Columbus
Hours 8am - 4:15pm
Description General Summary:
Work as part of a team of predictive modelers and analysts leveraging advanced analytics to drive innovation, inform strategy development, and enhance competitiveness for all lines of personal and business insurance.
Build and deliver predictive models and data mining applications for a variety of business functions throughout the enterprise.

Key Responsibilities
1. Extract large data volumes from various systems, perform quality checks, cleansing, and transformations in order to construct modeling-ready datasets.

2. Rigorously build predictive models of business outcomes such as claim frequency, customer retention, and agent performance, using SAS and other software.

3. Develop evidence of model validity, stability, and business value in reference to current practices.

4. Share findings, business implications, and action recommendations via written and oral presentations.

5. Provide clear technical documentation to facilitate knowledge transfer and retention.

6. Support implementation teams to put approved models into production; monitor existing models for accuracy and stability.

7. Investigate potential business value for new data sources, vendor products, and methodological enhancements.
Requirements Minimum Experience / Education:
 3+ years in predictive modeling and related analytics (Generalized Linear Models, Decision Tree, Spatial Analysis, Survival Analysis , Simulation, Optimization, etc.)

 Advanced degree (masters or PhD) in Applied Statistics, Operations Research, Econometrics, Decision Science, Applied Mathematics, or other rigorous quantitative discipline

Communication and Collaboration Skills:
 Outstanding oral and written communication skills; ability to create effective data visualizations

 Interpersonal skills, and inclination to interact regularly in direct collaboration with business partners across the enterprise

 Strong organizational skills to plan, balance, and maintain accountability for multiple concurrent projects



Technical Knowledge:
 In-depth knowledge of statistical techniques and theory, including multivariate statistical modeling.

 Skilled user of statistical modeling and data mining software (e.g., SAS).

 Proficiency in using developing complex data queries and analytic routines using programming languages (e.g., SQL)

Experience in property casualty insurance helpful but not required.
