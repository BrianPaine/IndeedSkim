    Data Analyst
    ID: 3390
    Location: Springfield, VA

    Description

    DESCRIPTION OF DUTIES
    Data Analytics support provides workforce analysis to inform resource decision making.  Through quantitative and qualitative data collection and analysis, achieves an analytical understanding of the actual requirements (e.g., positions) needed to support a Key Component (KC) functions to meet Agency’s mission, thereby supporting workload alignment efficiencies. Data analytics also use expertise and available resources to collect financial, survey and manpower data to inform and support PA&E studies, and provide data visualization that highlights and communicates the results of their analysis.
    Develop, design and organize research approaches for data collection and analysis; Administer quality control and process improvement activities, including data entry, data cleaning and data coding; Merge multiple datasets using advanced functions of Excel and other statistical applications; Develop queries and reports, extract data from PeopleSoft, Microsoft Access, Microsoft Excel, and SPSS, and analyze data using advanced statistical techniques; Design, conduct, analyze and report on quantitative and qualitative research; Display analyses results in text and visualizations to communicate results to customers; Support annual and ad hoc internal and external reporting requirements and prepare briefings and reports as needed.
    Conduct comprehensive manpower analyses to identify and validate total workforce requirements, support personnel/workload planning, and inform investment/divestment decision-making.
    Assist an Agency workforce analytic team in designing, creating, and conducting workload surveys and assessments across the Agency.
    Develop queries and reports, extracting and merging data from PeopleSoft, to determine total force population.
    Support brief to the organizations to be surveyed in order to prepare participating individuals.
    Monitor participation rates and engage with organizational leaders to ensure sufficient response rates.
    Consolidate survey results, and code and organize data for analysis.
    Conduct quality control discussions with supervisors to assess the overall consistency of individual survey results.
    Link large datasets and presenting data distributions in appropriate histograms, charts, graphs.
    Analyze survey results using descriptive and predictive analytics to assess workload gap and gap to vacancy correlations and other conclusions to ensure credibility in results.
    Collaborate in analysis discussions to assess the overall consistency of results.
    Display analyses results in text and visually using PPT, Excel, and other graph formats to communicate results (reports, briefings, spreadsheets, dashboards, position papers, email response to taskers, etc.)
    Assist the Agency in the presentation of survey results and with briefings to senior leaders on the task and products.
    Administer quality control and process improvements activities, including data entry, data cleaning, data coding, survey design, and use and presentation of analytics stemming from survey results and supporting data.
    Manage (retrieve, code, merge, organize) data using an intermediate to advanced knowledge of Excel, Access, SPSS.
    Perform in-depth research, analysis and recommendations for implementation of best practices and lessons learned which will improve Agency requirements determination analysis processes.
    Support ad hoc analysis and request from internal and external (ODNI) organizations.
    Attend meetings with partners related to data analysis and research projects.
    Participate in Agency, IC, and Federal Government wide working groups as directed.
    BASIC QUALIFICATIONS
    Bachelor's Degree with significant quantitative analytic focus.
    Minimum eight (8) years of experience and six (6) years of relevant experience in workforce/human capital analysis, business/finance/economics, operation research, and/or system engineering.
    Demonstrated experience in data collection and analysis.
    US Citizenship is required.  Applicants selected will be subject to a Government investigation and must meet eligibility requirements to obtain and retain the designated Government clearance (Top Secret).  Must have an active Top Secret/Special Compartmented Intelligence (SCI) clearance and a Counterintelligence Polygraph.
    PREFERRED QUALIFICATIONS
    Master degree with significant quantitative analytic focus.
    Proficiency with PeopleSoft, SPSS, Microsoft Access.
    Working knowledge of NGA organizations and mission.
    Working knowledge of Microsoft Project.
    Strong history/experience in customer-service, able to advise on tactical and strategic issues.
    Strong oral and written communication skills; demonstrated ability to develop written artifacts of the caliber to be consumed by working and senior audiences.
    Strong interpersonal skills; ability to work independently, and as a team member; ability to establish a network of contacts; ability to mentor and guide activities of other team members.
    Demonstrated ability to merge multiple workload and population datasets that involves use of advanced functions of Excel and other statistical applications.
    Demonstrated ability to conduct interviews, research, and data analysis to translate information into analytic results.
    Demonstrated proficiency in MS Office.
